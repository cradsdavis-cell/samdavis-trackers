What students see5 / 11
MATH5925 / DATA5925 · Group Project
Modelling ecoacoustic biodiversity on Llara Farm

The University of Sydney's DigiFarm initiative deployed 20 acoustic recorders across Llara Farm (Narrabri, NSW) from November 2019 to August 2021. Each recorder produced roughly 14{,}500 hourly observations, transformed into the Acoustic Diversity Index (ADI) — a proxy for avian species richness. One sensor has failed, and the project team needs a defensible recommendation for where to place its replacement.

Using the provided dataset:

  1. Clean the multi-sensor time-series and characterise its temporal structure. Identify the dominant seasonality and fit a forecasting model for each sensor. Diagnose the fit.
  2. Build a Gaussian Process regression model that predicts the spatial distribution of ADI across the farm with quantified uncertainty. Justify your kernel choice and report the posterior surface.
  3. Use the GP posterior to recommend an optimal location for the replacement sensor, via an acquisition function of your choice. Discuss how the uncertainty from (b) drives the decision in (c).

Stage 4

UCB Bayesian Optimisation — where to place the next sensor

Acquisition function $\text{UCB}(X) = \bar{f}^*(X) + 2\sigma_{f^*}(X)$, averaged across time. Green dot — the recommended next sensor location. Posterior uncertainty from Stage 3 is what makes this defensible.
